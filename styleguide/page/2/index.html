If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.

by:Albert Einsteinfrom:Everywhere on googlememo:The quote is not only for rocket-science things but marketing, management, education, and so on and so forth. He also said, “If I had only one hour to save the world, I would spend fifty-five minutes defining the problem, and only five minutes finding the solution.” Those quotes are well illustrated designers work. You have to distill the problem in the constrained time frame and solve it by making stuff that you think its clear, legible, and beautiful. Sounds painstaking? but I like that. You know this world today is a mess, and as a designer, I don’t want to add another mess or sloppy stuff. We have to keep in mind that we only have 5 minutes to solve the problem whenever we face it. We all learned from school’s summer homework that unprepare is the enemy. It makes you rush unhappily. But it’s never late than never. Just stop reading this memo or checking timeline on social-networks. Learn something from experts everyday, and add that essence to your work.

Typeface

Since lack of resource about typography in Japan, most people studying it through books written by Akira Kobayashi, Linotype’s in-house type designer, who is famously reworked the Avenir family with Adrian Frutiger. I was also one of a guy inspired by his book. I set in Avenir Next not because we have the family in latest OSX Mavericks, but the story and the process through improving the font from Avenir to Avenir Next, and more importantly, it’s legible but very soft.
